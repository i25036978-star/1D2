Administrative Divisions

Beijing is the capital of the People’s Republic of China, a directly-administered municipality, a national central city, a megacity, and a global first-tier city. Its total area is 16,410.54 square kilometers. Beijing is located in the northern part of the North China Plain, bordering Tianjin to the east and Hebei Province elsewhere.

As of October 2023, Beijing administers 16 municipal districts, with no counties or county-level cities - it was one of the first cities in China to achieve a “no-county”administrative structure. The 16 districts can be grouped into four functional tiers:

Capital Function Core District (2 districts): Dongcheng District and Xicheng District. Located within the Second Ring Road, this area roughly corresponds to the Ming and Qing imperial city. It concentrates the national political center, the protection of the historic city, and central government functions. In 2010, the State Council approved the merger of the former Dongcheng District and Chongwen District into the new Dongcheng District, and the merger of the former Xicheng District and Xuanwu District into the new Xicheng District.

Central Urban Area (the “Six Urban Districts” (4 districts): Chaoyang District, Haidian District, Fengtai District, Shijingshan District. Together with Dongcheng and Xicheng, they form the “Six Urban Districts,”the core functional area of Beijing. Chaoyang is the center for international exchange and the Central Business District (CBD). Haidian is the core of science, technology, and innovation, home to top universities such as Tsinghua and Peking University, as well as the Zhongguancun Science and Technology Park.

New Urban Development Zones (6 districts): Tongzhou District, Daxing District, Shunyi District, Changping District, Fangshan District, and Beijing Economic-Technological Development Area (part of Daxing). Among them, Tongzhou District hosts the Beijing municipal government and is being developed as the Beijing Municipal Sub-center.

Ecological Conservation Development Zones (4 districts): Mentougou District, Huairou District, Pinggu District, Miyun District, Yanqing District. These districts serve as ecological barriers and water source conservation areas. Miyun Reservoir is a major source of drinking water for Beijing.

Aviation Hubs

Beijing has a comprehensive transportation hub pattern of “eight stations and two airports”- the first city in China to form an integrated passenger transport network with eight Class-I railway passenger stations and two large international airports. The “two airports”are Beijing Capital International Airport and Beijing Daxing International Airport, both serving as international aviation hubs. In 2024, their combined passenger throughput reached approximately 117 million.

Capital Airport is located in Shunyi District in the northeastern part of Beijing. It was the first civilian international airport put into operation in the People’s Republic of China and is often called the “First Gateway of China.”The airport is about 25 kilometers from the city center. It has three terminals: T1 (closed for renovation), T2, and T3.

During the winter season from October 26, 2025 to March 28, 2026, Capital Airport plans an average of 1,243 scheduled flights per day, serving a total of 212 destinations, including 207 passenger destinations - 127 domestic (including Hong Kong, Macao, and Taiwan) and 80 international. Some Air China domestic flights have adjusted terminal assignments: 12 destinations (e.g., Zhangjiajie, Guangyuan, Jingdezhen, Nanchang, Taiyuan) moved from T2 to T3; 9 destinations (e.g., Xichang, Yibin, Shangrao, Korla, Yuncheng) moved from T3 to T2.

Main connections to the city: Capital Airport Express subway (Dongzhimen - T2/T3, separate fare), airport buses (multiple routes covering major city stops), and taxis/ride-hailing services.

Daxing Airport is located on the border between Daxing District, Beijing and Langfang City, Hebei Province. It opened on September 25, 2019, positioned as a “new power source for national development”and a large international aviation hub, as well as a landmark project for the coordinated development of the Beijing-Tianjin-Hebei region. The airport is about 46 kilometers from the city center. It has a main terminal and satellite halls connected by an internal automated people mover system.

During the winter season from October 26, 2025 to March 28, 2026, Daxing Airport plans an average of about 979 flights per day - approximately 897 domestic and 82 international and Hong Kong/Macao/Taiwan flights. It will serve 130 domestic passenger destinations and 40 international and regional destinations.

On international routes, China Southern Airlines launched a direct flight from Beijing Daxing to Doha, Qatar on October 16, 2025. China Eastern Airlines plans to open direct flights from Beijing Daxing to Muscat, Oman, as well as flights to Bali, Phu Quoc, Kobe, and Sapporo. China United Airlines plans to gradually open routes to Japan, Southeast Asia, and other directions.

Main connections to the city: Daxing Airport Express subway (Caoqiao - Daxing Airport, separate fare, 19-minute direct trip), Beijing-Xiong’an intercity railway, airport buses, and taxis/ride-hailing services.

Rail Transit

1. Conventional Subway Lines

These are the backbone of the network, operating underground, at grade, or on elevated structures. They serve the central urban area and extend into the suburbs. They include most numbered lines (e.g., Lines 1, 2, 4, 5, 6, 8, 9, 10, 13, 14, 15, 16, 17, 18, 19), as well as the Batong Line (integrated with Line 1), Daxing Line (integrated with Line 4), Fangshan Line, Changping Line, and Yizhuang Line.

2. Medium-Low Speed Maglev Line

The S1 Line is Beijing’s only medium-low speed maglev line, using electromagnetic force for levitation and propulsion. It connects Shijingshan and Mentougou districts, offering a quieter and smoother ride compared to conventional steel-wheel systems.

3. Modern Tram Lines

The Xijiao Line (Bagou - Fragrant Hills) and the Yizhuang T1 Line (within the Yizhuang area) fall into this category. They run mostly at grade and are integrated into the overall network, though separate fare rules may apply to some lines.

4. Airport Rail Links

The Capital Airport Express connects Dongzhimen with Terminals 2 and 3 of Capital Airport. The Daxing Airport Express connects Caoqiao with Daxing Airport. Both lines have separate fares and are not included in the standard distance-based subway fare system.

5. Suburban Express Lines (including Regional Rapid Rail)

These lines have longer station spacing and higher operating speeds, primarily providing rapid commuting between the central urban area and outer suburbs, as well as cross-regional services. This category includes the Yanfang Line (connecting Yanshan area with Fangshan Line), among others.

Beijing was the first city in China to open a subway, with its first line entering service on January 15, 1971. As of February 2026, the Beijing rail transit network comprises 30 lines, covering a total operating length of 909.0 kilometers, with 423 stations, including 106 interchange stations. The network includes Lines 1 through 19, as well as the Batong Line, Daxing Line, Fangshan Line, Changping Line, Yizhuang Line, Yanfang Line, S1 Line, Xijiao Line, Yizhuang T1 Line, Capital Airport Express, and Daxing Airport Express.

Ground Bus Transit

1. Overview

2,564 bus lines (as of 2025), about 21,000 vehicles (74% new energy). Average daily ridership approximately 6 million. 88% of bus stops are within 50 meters of a subway station.

2. Fares

Standard urban routes: flat fare 2 RMB (1 RMB with card/QR code). Suburban and long-distance routes: distance-based, 2-10 RMB. Express direct lines: 5-15 RMB. Using Beijing Municipal Administration & Communication Card (physical or NFC), Beijing Bus App, or Alipay/WeChat QR code gives 50% discount (25% for students). Free for seniors aged 65+ with senior card, and for children under 1.3 meters.

3. Route Types

· Urban routes (1-599): Mainly within the six core districts, short stop spacing, high frequency.

· Suburban routes (601-599, especially 800/900 series): Connect city center with outer suburbs, long distance, distance-based fare.

· BRT (BRT1-4): Dedicated lanes, high-capacity vehicles, flat fare 2 RMB (1 RMB with card/QR code).

· Micro-circulation routes (Zhuan prefix): Short-distance feeders to subway, communities, hospitals; small buses, fare 2 RMB (1 RMB with card/QR code).

· Express direct lines: Peak-hour service, limited stops, guaranteed seat, fare 5-15 RMB.

· Night routes (Ye prefix): Operate 23:20-04:50, cover major roads, fare 2 RMB (1 RMB with card/QR code).

· School / tourist specials: Fixed schedule, reservation required or operate on holidays; fare per ride or distance-based.

4. Operating Hours

Urban routes: first bus approx. 05:00-05:30, last bus approx. 22:00-23:00. Suburban routes: first bus approx. 05:30-06:00, last bus approx. 20:00–21:00. Night routes: headway 30–60 minutes. Check real-time information via mobile                              apps. 

5. Payment Methods

Beijing Municipal Administration & Communication Card (physical or NFC), Beijing Bus App, Alipay/WeChat QR code (scan both when boarding and alighting on distance-based routes). Cash accepted without change, no discount.

6. Major Hubs

Deshengmen West (for Yanqing, Changping), Dongzhimen (for Shunyi, Huairou), Sihui (for Tongzhou), Songjiazhuang (southern suburbs), Beijing West Railway Station South Square (southwest suburbs), Tiantongyuan North (northern suburbs).

7. Inquiry Tools

Beijing Bus App (real-time location, arrival alerts), Amap (Gaode), Baidu Maps, Tencent Maps (route planning), Beijing Traffic App (official), hotline 96166.

8. Boarding Rules

Board at front door, exit at rear door (for distance-based routes, tap card/QR code at both doors). Flammable/explosive items and pets (except guide dogs) prohibited. Priority seats for elderly, weak, pregnant, disabled. No eating on board.

Railway Hub

Beijing railway hub is at the core of the national rail network. In 2024, all railway stations in the Beijing area handled a total of 336 million passenger trips, averaging 918,000 per day.

The “eight stations”refer to the eight Class-I railway passenger stations in Beijing, each serving trains in different directions:

· Beijing Station, Beijing Chaoyang Station, Beijing Tongzhou Station - eastbound and northeastbound trains.

· Beijing North Station, Qinghe Station - northwestbound trains.

· Beijing West Station, Beijing Fengtai Station - southwestbound trains.

· Beijing South Station - southeastbound trains.

Among them, Beijing Tongzhou Station opened on December 30, 2025. Located within the Sub-central Center comprehensive transport hub, it is a fully underground station connected to Subway Line 6. These eight stations work in a coordinated division of labor, effectively dispersing passenger flow that was previously concentrated in the central urban area and reducing pressure on inner-city transport.
