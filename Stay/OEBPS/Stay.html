Accommodation Guide

Luxury Top-Tier Choices

Mandarin Oriental Qianmen, Beijing

Located in Caochang Hutong, Qianmen, this is Beijing’s only all-villa siheyuan (courtyard) hotel, with just 42 courtyard-style guest rooms. In 2025 it received the “Prix Versailles International Architecture Award”as the world’s most beautiful hotel. It blends hutong texture with Eastern aesthetics to perfection and is known as one of “China’s most expensive hotels.”

· Address: Caochang Hutong, Dongcheng District

Bulgari Hotel Beijing

Situated in the embassy district between Sanlitun and the Liangma River, designed by an Italian architecture firm and home to a Michelin-starred Italian restaurant. The guest rooms combine Italian luxury with the tranquility of Beijing’s embassy district, making it a synonym for fashionable luxury in the capital.

· Address: Building 2, No. 8 Xinyuan Nanlu, Chaoyang District

China World Summit Wing, Beijing

Occupying the 80th floor and above of the China World Tower III in the CBD, this is the highest luxury hotel in Beijing, offering a 360-degree panoramic view of the city. The 64th-floor sky lobby and 79th-floor French restaurant are city landmarks.

· Address: No. 1 Jianguomenwai Dajie, Chaoyang District

Aman Summer Palace

Adjacent to the East Palace Gate of the Summer Palace, with a dedicated entrance for hotel guests. The hotel was converted from Ming- and Qing-style courtyard buildings and received a Michelin Key in 2025. Staying here feels like stepping back into a royal palace of a century ago.

· Address: No. 1 Gongmenqianjie, Summer Palace, Haidian District

Xitan Hotel Beijing

Located in Tanzhe Temple Town, Mentougou District, hidden in the Western Hills. Designed by a Japanese landscape team, it has only 38 guest rooms, some with private hot spring baths -one courtyard, one household, one private bath. It is the pinnacle of mountain resort stays in Beijing.

· Address: Yangpo Yuan Village, Tanzhe Temple Town, Mentougou District

Historic & State Guest Choice

Beijing Hotel

Founded in 1900 as a small French-owned bistro, located at the south entrance of Wangfujing on East Chang’an Avenue. The founding banquet of the People’s Republic of China was held here. In 2008, it served as the headquarters hotel for the Olympic Family during the Beijing Olympics. It carries 125 years of state memories and is known as “the No.1 time-honored hotel of China”.

· Address: No. 33 East Chang’an Avenue, Dongcheng District

Siheyuan (Courtyard) Culture Choices

Shichahai Zitan Cultural Theme Hotel

Converted from the former residence of Sony, a high official during the Kangxi reign of the Qing dynasty, preserving the traditional siheyuan layout. Guest rooms are furnished with precious solid wood furniture such as rosewood and golden nanmu, offering a perfect fusion of classical Chinese culture and modern living.

· Address: No. 42 Xinghua Hutong, Xicheng District

Beijing Stay B&B (Zhuzhu Youlan Hotel)

A siheyuan hidden in Dengshikou Hutong, Wangfujing, blending a Japanese dry landscape garden with private hot spring baths. Quiet amidst the bustle, it offers a unique experience for travelers seeking “urban seclusion”.

· Address: near Dengshikou Street, Dongcheng District (check booking platforms for exact location)

Le Zai Nanluo Siheyuan Boutique Hotel

Located in Shajing Hutong, Nanluoguxiang, with guest rooms named after the 24 solar terms. More than half of the rooms have private courtyards. Its neo-Chinese design is elegant and relaxing, creating a small, beautiful urban oasis in a century-old hutong.

· Address: No. 3 Shajing Hutong, Nanluoguxiang, Dongcheng District

Budget Accommodation Choices

Economy Chain Hotels

Hutong Youth Hostels

Budget economy chain hotels in Beijing are numerous. Brands such as Home Inn, Hanting, and 7 Days have over 300 locations citywide, most near transport hubs or scenic spots, offering reliable cleanliness. Prices typically range from 200 to 400 RMB per night. Below are representative branches with good guest reviews:

Hanting Hotel Beijing Qianmen Dashilan -located in the Qianmen core area, a 5-minute walk from Qianmen Subway Station (Line 2). Average nightly rate in 2023 was about 387 RMB. Offers free Wi-Fi, luggage storage, and buffet breakfast -a good balance of value and convenience.

Home Inn Select Beijing Qianmen Pedestrian Street -a 10-minute walk to Tian’anmen Square, surrounded by dining and shopping options. Jinjiang Inn Beijing Qianmen Subway Station -good value, convenient, close to the subway. GreenTree Inn Beijing Qianmen Dashilan Express Hotel -about 350-450 RMB per night, a 10-minute walk to Tian’anmen Square.

In addition, mid-range brands like Atour and Ji Hotel are also affordable in some areas. For example, Atour X Hotel Beijing Yonghegong is just a few minutes-walk from the Lama Temple and highly rated by guests.

If you want to deeply experience old Beijing’s hutong life, youth hostels are the best choice. Hostel prices in Beijing typically range from 50 to 120 RMB per bed. They are mainly concentrated in three areas:

Nanluoguxiang / Gulou Area -the first choice for artistic youth. Most hostels are converted from traditional siheyuan. Staying in a hutong, you might wake up to the sound of pigeon whistles, and open your window to grey tile roofs. Transport advantage: intersection of Subway Lines 6 and 8. Recommended: Beijing Piao International Youth Hostel, Paoju Factory Youth Hostel (located in a hutong behind Yonghegong, converted from a Qing-dynasty cannon factory, unique style). Beds about 70-120 RMB/night.

Qianmen / Dashilan Area -the best choice if you want to watch the flag-raising ceremony at Tian’anmen Square, just a 10-minute walk away. Most hostels are concentrated around Meishi Street and Yangmeizhu Xiejie, preserving old Beijing architectural features. Transport advantage: direct access to Qianmen Subway Station (Line 2). After watching the flag raising, you can go back to the hostel for a nap. Recommended: Xin Yan Du Qianmen Branch, hostels around Beijing Fun. Beds about 80-150 RMB/night.

Dongsi / Beixinqiao Area -compared to the tourist-crowded Nanluoguxiang, this area has more local street life, many food options, and the most affordable prices. Beixinqiao Luzhu (braised offal stew) is open 24 hours, and Guijie night market is within walking distance. Transport advantage: dual coverage of Beixinqiao Station (Line 5) and Dongsi Station (Line 6), convenient to the Lama Temple, Temple of Heaven, and Wangfujing. Beds about 50-90 RMB/night.

Area Recommendations

· Dongcheng District (Tian’anmen / Wangfujing) : Central attractions concentrated, subway convenient. Suitable for first-time visitors who want to walk to the Forbidden City, Tian’anmen, and Wangfujing. Rich selection of high-end hotels and boutique siheyuan.

· Xicheng District (Shichahai / Nanluoguxiang) : Strong hutong culture, suitable for artistic youth and families wanting an in-depth experience of old Beijing’s lively atmosphere.

· Chaoyang District (CBD / Sanlitun) : International business district, abundant shopping and entertainment. Suitable for business travelers, young people, and shopping lovers.

· Haidian District (Zhongguancun / Summer Palace) : Cluster of universities and tech companies. Suitable for university or tech tours, adjacent to the Summer Palace and Old Summer Palace.

· Fengtai District / Tongzhou District : Relatively cheaper, close to Beijing West Railway Station and Beijing South Railway Station. Suitable for budget travelers and transit passengers. In Tongzhou, standard rooms near Jiukeshu or Liyuan are about 180-350 RMB/night.

· Book in advance : During peak season (May to October), book at least 2 weeks ahead. For holidays, book 1 month ahead. Hostel beds are especially competitive.

· Check transport : Use a map app to measure the actual walking distance from the hotel to the subway station. Anything over 800 meters will feel very long after a full day of sightseeing.

· Avoid pitfalls : Small, cheap hotels near railway stations may be of poor quality. Some hotels in the old city have outdated facilities - check recent guest reviews.

· Holiday surge : During holidays, average hotel prices in Beijing rise by more than 60%, and some popular areas may have limited availability.
